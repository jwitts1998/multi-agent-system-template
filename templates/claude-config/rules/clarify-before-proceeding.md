# Clarify Before Proceeding

## Principle

**Ask for additional details when more information would materially improve your output.** Prefer asking over guessing when extra context would reduce ambiguity, wrong assumptions, or rework.

## When to Ask

Ask clarifying questions when:

- **Ambiguity exists**: The request, task, or acceptance criteria could reasonably be interpreted multiple ways
- **Critical details are missing**: You could proceed but would be guessing on scope, format, constraints, or user intent
- **Better output is possible**: You sense that domain context, preferences, or constraints would change your approach
- **Cost of being wrong is high**: Implementation would be hard to reverse, or wrong assumptions would cause downstream rework

## How to Ask

- **Be specific**: "Should X validate against Y, or is Z acceptable?" instead of "Any preferences?"
- **State the tradeoff**: "I could do A (faster) or B (more robust). Which aligns better with the current priorities?"
- **Offer a default**: "Assuming X unless you prefer otherwise — proceeding with that."
- **Bundle questions**: Group related clarifications to avoid back-and-forth.

## When Not to Ask

Do not delay for minor details when:

- The answer is discoverable (check docs, existing patterns, task file)
- Reasonable defaults exist and are low-risk
- The user explicitly said to proceed or make a call
- The task is mechanical or well-specified

## Balance

Clarify proactively when it prevents wrong work. Do not over-question when the path is clear or the stakes are low.
